The Repertory Company of the National School of Drama was formed on an experimental basis by Shri E. Alkazi, who played a major role in nurturing and expanding the
Cornpary when he was Director of the School (1962-77), In the begining, the Company
consisted of only four actors, it later expanded into a larger group of actors rnusinians

and teachicians, mainly graduates of the School. Initially, in 1964, the late Om Shivpuri led the Company and later in 1977 Shri Manohar Singh was appointed the first
chief of the company. Over the years the Reportory has produced a variety of plays
ranging from stylised musicals to realistic contemporary works of Indian drama to

translations and adaptations of foreign language plays.
Eminent directors from within the country and abroad have worked with the company
in the past years.
Apart from performing in Delhi, the company tours extensively all over India and has
also made successful trips to Germany, Poland, British, Nepal and Mauritius.
