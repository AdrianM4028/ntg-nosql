“The Typists" deals with the life of two typists employed by a company
where they are required to type out names and addresses of prospective

costumers from the telephone directory on one side of the postcard and
advertisements on the other. These are then sent to the prospective
customers. Anil Bhandari, a new recruit, is an ambitious young man whose

aim in life is to become a Chartered Accountant. Meenakshi Ghosh. also
working at the office, helps him get alongwit h the work. They are both around
twenty years of age. The revelation of A nil , being a married man, is not taken

well by Meenakshi. With the passage of time, Anil and Meenakshi get
emotionally closer, but circumtances and the set up prevent their relationship. from moving any further.
What is it that plays upon their minds ’? They are victims of their
environment and heredity which have weakened and turned them into

objects of self pity. They do not have enough courage to rise abvoe the
situation they are in. Their tragic lifestyle does make one laugh, but then
are'nt we all trapped in similar conditions, today ’? These two are failures
even when they try to be escapists. But is it not natural ’? They look to each
other to break free of the shackels that bind them but none has the courage
to do so himself or herself.

The play is a tragic comedy and the playwright gives an insight into the
material and practical side of life, realistically. Novel and inventive theatrical
devices have been used with Time Space and Action. The play spans a
period of their lifetime. At times ifa character makes an entry preceded by
an exit, he or she grows older in their respective ages marked by subtle
changes in the character. Thus, time keeps changing but their work and the
place of work. i.e., the action and space remains unaltered.
