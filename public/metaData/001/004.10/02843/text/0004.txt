to cover man versus science and technology. In
the play, the character ‘Vibhuti’ symbolises the
modern machinery, which in turn stands as a

symbol for science and technology. The king of
Dehra uses ‘Bodh Yantra’ , i.e., machines, to
conquer and torture the residents of his
neighbouring kingdom of Auranga. The play
seeks to bring out the inhuman side of science
and technology and its usage, and serves as a
kind of warning to developed nations to stop
using the ‘Bodh Yantra’ or machine for their
selﬁsh ends. The play seeks to establish that
machines were made for the beneﬁt of man,
and not the other way round. Science and technology should be used for the beneﬁt of mankind, rather than for its destruction.
For the ﬁrst time the topic of science and
technology has been taken up in the Bidesia
theatre form, thereby making a folk art form
deal with a very contemporary theme. It also for
the ﬁrst time makes an essentially rural form
deal with an urban problem.
In the Bedisia style of acting the emphasis
is not so much on scenic design as it is on acting.
Through their acting the characters create
scenes. The Director has substituted the names
of places and incidents with those of Bihar, so
that they are in harmony with the use of Bhojpuri
and Magahi dialects. Hindi is used only in court
scenes. Bhojpuri is used by the residents ofDehra
and Magahi is used by the residents of Auranga
in the play.

The character Bairagi symbolises the philosophy of non-violence of Mahatma Gandhi, and
his role in organising the residents of Auranga
against the exploitation by the King of Dehra
symbolises the role of Gandhiji in the freedom
struggle.
