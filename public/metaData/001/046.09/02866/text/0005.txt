GornbroWicz is a leading representative of
dramatists of the grotesque even though his most
important plays were written before the genre
became popular. His central theme is life's
enslavement by “form", which he deﬁnes as a
manner of being or mode of behavior that is
imposed upon man either by society or by his
upbringing. The more forms that appear in
Gornbrowicz's dramatic parodies, the more
grotesque and absurd the action.

In Princess Ivona (wr. 1936, prod. 1957),
Ivona is totally devoid of form. She says and
does nothing, thus forcing others to adopt a
position toward her that invariably becomes
incongruous because she fails to play an active
role. This provokes those involved with her to
play progressively more ridiculous roles until
they are made painfully aware of their own
obsurdity. For the sake of form and society, Ivona
must be eliminated.
