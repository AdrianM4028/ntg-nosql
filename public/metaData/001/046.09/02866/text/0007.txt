all that Gombrowicz has penned—even in his

conﬂict, dialogue. Everything is the stage. The
production is both the process and the subject.
The characters he creates have an immediate
physical presence even if they are a product of
the action itself or the consequences of a chance
decision, often leading a character or a group of
people into a whirlpool of events. There is, thus,
confrontation without respite. Processions of
masks are framed, ideas enter into play, into an
encounter or the travesty of an encounter. The
action may be full of intrigue, but there is also
laughter, ceremony, a real duel or a duel of
shadows.
Another theatrical effect is the splitting of
characters, actions or discourse which produces
a grotesque impact, either laughter or shudder.
Everything, thus, is drama : conﬂicts,
ambiguities, relationships between the mask and
the face, between the individual and the puppet,
between the manipulator and the manipulated.
