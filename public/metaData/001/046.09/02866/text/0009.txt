If Gombrowicz had intended to bring us back
from the spiritual to the physical, from the
maturity of forms ossified around youth which
crushes them, we would then be confronted with
an idea even if it is an ancient one. But the author
succeeds in disturbing us, in making us feel
uneasy. For the idea which he evokes becomes
part of the intrigue and gets bogged down.
Gombrowicz adored trivial forms—provincial
novel, Gothic novel, Operetta—and is conscious
of “high culture". He combines Goethe with
Jorry. He plays the insurmountable tension

between the real Self and the Self I drive at
having gone through the others.
