The characters he creates have an immediate physical presence
even they are a prod u ct of the action itself or the consequences
of a chance decision, often leading a character or a group of
people into a whirlpool of events. There is, thus, confrontation
without respite. Processions of masks are framed, ideas enter
into play, into an encounter or the travesty of an encounter.

The action may be full of intrigue, but there is also laughter,
ceremony, a real duel or a duel of shadows.
Another theatrical effect is the splitting of characters, actions
or discourse which produces a grotesque impact either

laughter or shudder.
Everything, thus, is drama: conflicts, ambiguities, relationships
between the mask and the face, between the individual and

the puppet, between the manipulator and the manipulated.
