In Princess Ivona (wr. 1936, prod. 1957), Ivona is totally
devoid of form. She says and does nothing, thus forcing
others to adopt a position toward her that invariably becomes
incongruous because she fails to play an active role. This
provokes those involved with her to play progressively more
ridiculous roles until they are made painfully aware of their
own absurdity. For the sake of form and society, Ivona must
be eliminated.
