About the Play
Eccentric, non—conformist, idiosyncratic, idealistic; that's Mr. Shambhu
Nath Mehta, a man who went to work one morning, reached the 10th floor

of his office and came back down in the elevator. Having opted out of the
rat-race because he realized that he was missing out on the simple
pleasures of life, he now presides over a household of equally eccentric

individuals who all live by a laissez faire creed and follow their own dreams.
There is his daughter, Vidya, who accidentally took to playwriting when a
typewriter was delivered to the house by mistake. Vidya's husband, Arun
Mukherjee, who loves to tinker with firecrackers along with D. P.Sin ha who

came to deliver gas one day and never left the family since. His elder
granddaughter Neha who makes candles and loves to dance, even though
she stinks at it. Neha's husband, Aslarn Pervez, who prints random material

and plays the keyboard with little success. Then there is Vinnie, the
younger granddaughter, the only one in the house who holds a steady job
in the outside world and has her feet firmly planted in the ground.
