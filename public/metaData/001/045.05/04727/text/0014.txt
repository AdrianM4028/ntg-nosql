What the director wants to say is actually said through his production only. This creation of
Arthur Miller—Death of a Salesman, has a unique position in Would Drama. It positions itself in
the all time great classics. Modern theatre philosophers and intellectuals consider it to be the
most modern of all modern tragedies. In today’s world of cut-throat competition and an
insensitive set-up and system, human relations are becoming more and more complex and
distorted. Each one of us, in our Ionliness, has faced it and is struggling against it also.
The Economic System is killing our emotional fibres also—there is no place for any
helplessness or weakness. In this madening rush for development and with increasing
consumerism becoming all pervasive, this creation of Arthur Miller becomes contemporary and
relevant to our society. Despite being an American Play, today, it speaks the story of our own

society. Besides, a ‘Common Man’ is the protagonist of this tragedy.
