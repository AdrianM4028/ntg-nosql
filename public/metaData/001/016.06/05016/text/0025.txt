The speciality of this play is that it supplements and complements the two different stories oftwo poets. A spirit of light
humour keeps the audience in a joyful mood throughout the show besides making them think seriously about the
issues involved. Parimala a character in the play is brought up by her step mother after the demise of her father is
bestowed with the lot of wealth, arrogance and youthful looks. The secretary Shiromani who was incharge of the
ﬁnancial transactions of the family right from the days of Parimala's father succeeded in betraying the stepmother by
getting all the property transferred in the name of Parimala and continued to support her. The will speciﬁed that
Parimala will not be entitled to enjoy the property unless she gets married. To overcome this condition, Shiromani
traps Parimala in a situation where she marries an illiterate, poor and an henpecked husband, Nani - Narayana Rao,
who carries on with this pretension. The servant Rama appraises him of the famin secrets at the apporpriate time.
Both of them show Parimala the true nature of Shiromani and be removing the ignorance and arrongance, they bring
about a total change in the family.
Educated Bhoopalaraya who had led a full and complete life lost everything and
had to bear the burden of his loans. After losing everything Bhoopala left his
home town and came to this town without food, shelter and clothing. He went to
Parimala's house and was insulted. lrnpressed with his talk, Rama provides him
with shelter. Bhoopala plans to teach a lesson to the rich people who have
earned black money and he decides to distrubute it among the poor people. He
announces to give 50 to 60% interest on the money who invest with him. He
uses money of the rich to the welfare of the poor people. Since Bhoopala had
executed his plan with great foresight and within the limits of law, the black
marketeers realised that Bhoopala had taken away their money. They could not
take any action against him as they were relieved to escape from the clutches of
law. In order to repay the debt of Rama, Bhoopala returns Parimala's money
given by Shiromani to her. He brings Pankaiakshi home and sees that the
stepmother, husband Nani live together happily. Bhoopala distributes the illegal
money among the poor and leaves the town.
