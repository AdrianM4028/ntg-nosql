The locale of this story is set in a small village of
masons and bricklayers. This is the story of their
lives as told by them.

Scene 1 . Faqir Darya Shah unravels the intricate
webs of illusions that confound his disciple
Qatra Shah, explaining that wealth and woman
cast a spell on mortal man and blind him to
truth.

Two brothers from a nearby village pass by. Pira
is a simple and honest human being, while his
brother Mira is full of lust for worldly
possessions. They are both looking for work.

Darya Shah asks Pira to build a shelter for
travelers on the Takia and offers him half-wages.
Pira agrees and accepts the purse of money from
the Faqir.
Qatra Shah is still confused. Darya Shah advises
him to remain detached from worldly passions
for that is the only way to realization.

Scene 2. Plagued by his creditor Alijan, Mira
begs his brother to lend him some money from
the Faqir’s purse. But Pira’s sense ofjustice will
not allow it.
