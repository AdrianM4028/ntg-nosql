Arnal Allana, one of the well

known young stage-directors of
Delhi, graduated from the National
School of Drama as the best All
Round Student-Director in ’68.
After her graduation she spent two
years at the Berliner Ensemble,
G.D.R., as an observer, and later

visited Japan to study the Japanese
Theatre. She was Head of the
Department of Indian Theatre at
Punjab University from ’77 to ’78.
Amal Allana has formed two
theatre groups: The Workshop
in Bombay (’71) and Studio 1 in
Delhi (’78). She was granted the
Horni Bhabha fellowship for
research in evolving new forms of
contemporary Indian Theatre as
related to our own folk traditions.

over thirty plays, some of which
