The Repertory
Company

The Repertory Company of the
National School of 'Drama is a
performing group of players and
technicians who have graduated
from the school in the past years.
Formed in 1964 with just four
actors, the Company was started
on an experimental basis by
E. Alkazi, the Director of the

School till the late 708, and a
dynamic theatre personality who
had a major hand in nuturing
and expanding the Company
over the years.

In 1977 when the Company

vauired the status of National
Repertory Shri Manohar Singh, took
over as its Chief and Carried
its activities across the nation, the
foundation for which had been laid
by Shri E. Alkaji.
Since the yearly ’70s the Company
has produced a rich variety of plays

both classical and contemporary,
in several Indian languages.
Not tied to any particular style of
performance the Company has
produced plays that range from
stylised musicals to realistic
contemporary works. A few
examples are: Tughlaq, Andha Yug,

Chhote Saiyad Bade Saiyad,
Panchvan Savar, Rasgandharva,
Surya Ki An tim Kiran Se Surya Ki
Pehli Kiran Tak, Charpai,
Sainyan Bhaye Kotwal, Adhe
Adhure, Sandhya Chhaya, Jasma

Odan Mattavilas, and Viraasat.
The Repertory has worked with
a variety of directors from India
and aborad. Besides using the best
available scripts the company has
also successfully dramatised and
produced a few novels and short
stories in Hindi as Teen Ekant
Begum Ke Takia, Mukhya Mantri,
and Mahabhoj. In the past years
its repertories has also included
foreign plays in translations/
adaptations. Some examples are
Brecht’s The Caucasion Chalk

Circle The Three Penny Opera and
Puntila And His Man Matti.
Shakespeare’s Othelo and Macbeth,
Moliere’s School For Wives,

Osborne’s Look Back In Anger,
Bond’s The Fool, Chekhov’s

Uncle Vanya and The Cherry
Orchard, Pirandello’s Six

Characters In Search of an Author,
Orkeny’s Totek Rostand’s
Cyrano de Bergerac, Goldoni’s

The Servant of Two Masters.
The Company is based in New
Delhi where it gives regular
performances. It has a small Studio

Theatre and on Open-air Theatre
which are regularly used for
rehearsals and performances during
the theatre season.
Apart from giving performances
in Delhi the company undertakes
extensive tours to different parts of
India each year. It has also made
a successful tour of East Germany.
Britain and Poland with Jasma
Odan and Tughlaq Recently the
company undertook a tour of
GDR with its new production of
And/ta Yug.
