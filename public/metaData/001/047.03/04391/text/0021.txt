About the Play
Brabantio, a Senator and father of Desdemona, is
awakened in the night by Iago and Roderigo and told that
his daughter has eloped with Othello. He sets off with a
strong guard to arrest him.
Iago warns Othello of Brabanio's anger. Cassio comes
with an urgent summons from the Duke to his council. A
clash with Brabantio and his followers seems imminent,
but eventually the whole party goes off to the Duke's
council.
The Duke and the Senators are discussing the intentions
of the Turks with respect to Cyprus. Brabantio's charge
against Othlello of using witchcraft or drugs to win
Desdemona is refuted by both of them and dismissed by
the Duke. Othello is to go that night to Cyprus, and
Desdemona is given permission to come after him in the
care of Iago. Left alone with Rodeigo, Iago persuades
him to enlist and to go to Cyprus in disguise. lago begins
to reveal his true nature.
Cassio arrives from sea the and is followed in a second
vessel by Desdemona, Emilia, Iago, and Roderigo.
Anxiety is felt for the fate of Othello, till he arrives in a third
vessel. At Cyprus they come to know that a storm has
destroyed the Turkish fleet. Othello orders a general
celebration.
