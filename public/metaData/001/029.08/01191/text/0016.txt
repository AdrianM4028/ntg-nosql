Shakespeare, in this delightful comedy, transports his audiences into a realm offantasy. In his inimitable style he
weavesfour sub-plots into a single intriguing play.
The play revolves around the two pairs of lovers-Hermia
and Lysander, Helena and Demetrius, their hopes, misun-

derstandings and re-union .

Puck, the sprightly elfacts as a catalyst between the pairs.

The plot isfurther complicated by the machinations ofOberon
and Titania— thefairy King and Queen, who are involved in
a conﬂict over a page.
Bottom, in a donkey’s mask, adds a hilarious note to the

play, bringing it to a humorous climax.
Thus ends thefascinating, extremely enjoyable play, stimu-

lating the audiences to explore the eternal appeal of
Shakespeare.
