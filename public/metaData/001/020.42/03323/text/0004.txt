'Asmita' stands committed to aesthetically innovative and socially relevant
theatre. It takes up contemporary issues to underline the contours of our time
' while providing the best of entertainment. So far the group has staged Bhishrna
Sahni's "Hanoosh" (26 shows), Albert Camus’ 'Caligula' (21 shows),
Raghuvir Chaudhary's 'Sikandra Sani (1 at Urdu Academy Festival-93),
Dr. Girish Karnad's "Tughlaq' (17 shows), 'Rakt-Kalyan' (37 shows),
Dr. Dharamvir Bharti's 'Andhayug' (9 shows), Brecht's Exception & the
Rule (street play) Shakespeare's 'Julius Caeser' (8 shows) & Merchant of

Venice (as 'Dilfarosh' at Urdu Academy‘s festival '96), Eugene O'Neil's
Desire under the Elms (14 shows), Swadesh Deepak’s Court-Martial
(53 shows), Narendra Mohan's Qalander (Urdu Academy's Festival 1997),

Dario Fo's Operation Three Star (26 shows) Mahesh Dattani's Final
Solutions (22 shows) and Ashok Lal's Ek Mamooli Aadrni (25 Shows)
Swadesh Deepak's Sabse Udaas Kavita (8 shows) Shakespare’s Julius
Caeser restaged in 1998 (2 shows). Brecht's Kanpur Ki Aurat Bhali Ramkali
(11 Shows) and "Log Baag" a play by Neil Simon (5 shows). All under the
Direction of Aravind Gaur.
In support of people’s struggle against victimisation, “Asmita” gave streetshows of Safdar Hashmi’s Aurat (36 shows) and Chicago 1886 (78 shows),
Sanjha Jahan (21 shows) and Tasveer (17 shows).
As an umbrella organisation to healthy theatre activities, "Asmita" organised
workshops at different colleges in Delhi which led to the production of Brecht's
The Good Person of Setzuan at l.P. College, Bhisharn Sahni's Madhavi

at Gargi College, Dario Fo's Accidental Death of an Anarchist at School of
Planning & Architecture and Arthur Miller’s Crucible at |.P. College. The
group organised the two-and-half hour long solo performance of Piyush Mishra
which enthralled the audience at Jaipur and Calcutta also.

In recognition of its endeavours, the group has been invited to perform Court

Martial as the only entry in Hindi at National Drama Festival at Calicut
(Kerala), 'Asmita' has also performed 'Court Martial' at Dibrugarh (Chayanika),
Assam and Jarnrnu & Kashmir, 'Final Solutions' in Jabalpur and Gwalior

'Operation Three Star' in Begu Sarai (IPTA Golden Jubilee Festival),
Shakespeare's "Julius Caesar' at Dibrugarh and Sibsagar (Assam) for the
National Shakespeare Festival organised by Chayanika, scenes from Julius
Caesar, Sabse Udaas Kavita and Rakt Kalyan at the Prithvi International
Theatre Festival (November 1998). Also performed Rakt Kalyan on the
occasion of Girish Karnad being bestowed the Kalidaas Samrnan and
Gyanpeeth Award. Also staged "Ek Marnooli Aadmi" for Bhartiya Rang
Mahotsava (1999).
