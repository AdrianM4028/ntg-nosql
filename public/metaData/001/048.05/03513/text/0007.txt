Suresh Bhardwaj
Suresh Bhardwaj, passed out of the National School
of Drama (NSD) with the Diploma of Integrated
Course in Theatre Arts, was awarded the NSD
fellowship to work with the newly—established
Extension Programme Department in Theatre
Academies in 198.81. He became the technical
director at Bharat Bhawan, Bhopal in 1981. He
formed Sambhav, a theatre group in Delhi and created
an ensemble of artists, who, as of today, are well
known for their outstanding achievements in both
theatre and cinema. He then shifted emphasis to
films, television and cinema in 1985 as a writer,
director and scenographer. Besides, he is well known
for his contribution in the field of stage lighting and
has more than 200 plays to his credit. Suresh joined
NSD as a visiting faculty in 1984 and was appointed
as a regular faculty in 1 989 as lecturer ofstage lighting.
He worked in various positions of this premier
institute — Associate Professor (1996), Dean,

Academies (2000—02), and at present, he is the

Professor, Extension Programmes. He has also traveled
many countries for conducting workshops and
directing plays.
