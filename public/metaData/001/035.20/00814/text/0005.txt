AHALYAA
The literal meaning of the name Ahalyaa is "unploughed and unblemished, one
without shortcomings or defects."
Created from the mind of Brahma, the Creator, Ahalyaa incorporates the vast
emptiness of Space within herself. Herself a great seer, this noble and enlightened
woman received only indifference from her husband, the sage Gautama. Indra, chief of
the gods, appeared to her in the form of the sage Gautarna, and Ahalyaa accepted him
as she was firm in her devotion to Dharma (Duty). They were blissfully united. Aha l yaa
was surprised and pained by sage Gautama's anger and hurt at her "infidelity". Instead
of reacting with anger or rebellion, she with—drew into herself and become lost in
meditation, which made her aura so brilliant that even Sri Rama was attracted towards

her flame-like purity.

Ahalya is venerated for incorrupability. and invincibility of the Atman (Spirit).
