D RAUPAD I
Born from the sacrificial fire, this daughter of King Drupada was famous for her
peerless beauty. Also known as Panchaali, her birth and life coincided with times which
were notorious for disregard and disrespect for women. She sufferred repeated
humiliations at the hands of unjust and immoral men, but not without calamitious
consequences for them. She raised her voice and her strongest objections to the
demeaning and degrading norms, rituals and misinterpreted traditions which made a
woman a mere object of possesion in male hands. Her bold and penetrating intelligence
posed questions in the court of the Kurus, questions which remained unanswered but
resulted in bloodshed, fratricide and destruction.

The one and only female friend of Sri Krishna, perhaps his greatest, Draupadi
carried the burden of the task of caring for five husbands through mostly bad times. She
was the energising force beind their unity and actions.

Draupadi is a shining example of the autonomy of the Atman (Spirit), indomitable
and uncomprornised.
