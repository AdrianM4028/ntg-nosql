About the Play

BHAGWAD AJJUKKAM is a classical Sanskrit play written b y B odhayan and set in the backdrop of
traditional India. But we, here, at Literacy India have made it universal and contemporary by adapting it in
a different way. Keeping in mind that children are performing the play, various changes in the script have
been made. Music in the play is a Fusion of traditional and modem forms, which gives an additional
dimension to the comic element of the play. Similarly, different types of colourful costumes taken from
different forms further add a new flavour to the play.
‘ ’

The story of the play is unusual, usually not taken up to be performed by children but the innocence of the
children towards the thematic concerns enhances the comedy in the play. The play has been specially
designed to cater to audience of all ages.
The story of the play revolves around. 2 characters —— Guru and the Shishya whose activities are intervened
by the presence of a damsel, Vasantsena, who creates a stir in their lives. Shishya is bestotted by VasantSena’s beauty; she is bitten by a snake; her death is imminent but the Guru demonstrates his power by
giving his soul to Vasant-Sena; Vasant Sena’s awakening with Guru’s soul in her body leading to changes
in her behavior further makes the scene quite hilarious. Yamdoot (From the world of Death) who caused
confusion by taking a wrong soul adds on to the humour of the whole play.
