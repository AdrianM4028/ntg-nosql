The poet Pandit Radheshyam Kathawachak
was born in Bareli in 25th November 1890. His
father Pandit Bankelal was a kathawachak by
profession and used to narrate Tulsidas’s
Ramayan and Rukmini mangal with great zeal.

Thus Radheshyam got a taste of this “narration
style" from his childhood. But he added a new
innovation to that style. He invented his own
tune and metre and wrote ‘ ‘Radheshyam
Ram ayan " .

Those were the days of the freedom struggle
and new social reforms. Being a storyteller with
a vision, he easily understood the pulse of his
time. In most of his plays he had portrayed the
demons to be the British rulers.
His play “Veer Abhimanyu” was an attempt
which began a new chapter in the history of
Parsi Theatre. Not only was it the first “Parsi Style” play written exclusively in Hindustani,
but it also had constituents like soliloquie, the
presence of the Nat and Nati, the Bharat - Vakya,
and the incidence of Parikshit being crowned

the next prince; which would bring relief after
the incidence of Abhimanyu’s death. All these
constituents being characteristic of the Sanskrit
drama which believed in a “happy ending”

Lucid language, songs, and memorable dialogues were the salient features of Radheshyam’s
writings. Amongst his other works are plays
like Shrawan Kurnar, Bharat Mata, Bhakta
Prahlad, Pariwartan, Mashriki Hur, Shrikrishna
Awatar, Usha Anirudh, Sati Parwati, Shakuntala,
Draupadi Swayamwar, etc.
