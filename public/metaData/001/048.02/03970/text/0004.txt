In this golden iubilee year we have sought to take performance outside
the confines of auditoria into spaces that require a renegotiation of narrative
with location, and of location with design. I857, Ek Safarnama, directed

by Nadira Zaheer Babbar, was staged against the ramparts of the Purana
Quila. In the open lawns of the Indira Gandhi National .Centre for the Arts
Main Istanbul Hoon, the second such experiment, aims at plotting landscape

garden and field into the performance; the play itself moves from past to '
present and from imaginary space to real space in an instant, these shift are
sometimes smooth and at other times iagged and disconcerting.
Part of the of the project in seeking out new performance spaces is
also connected with finding new ways of making a theatrical experience
from non dramatic texts - Mohan Maharishi’s finely crafted Main Istanbul
Hoon is a rendering prompted from many different ways of telling stories
— autobiography, fiction, memoir, diary.
We are very happy that that Main Istanbul Hoon has been mounted in
collaboration with the Indira Gandhi Centre for the Arts; we hope we can
engage in more conversations of this sort in the future.
