INTRODUCTION
The Repertory Company of the National School of Drama
was formed on experimental basis by Shri. E. Alkazi, who

had major hand in nurturing and expanding the Company
as the Director of the School till mid 1970’s. In the
beginning, the Company—Consisted of four actors which
later expanded into a larger group of actors musicians and
technicians, mainly the graduates from the School. Initially

in I 964 late Om Shivpuri was leading the Company and
later in 1977 Shri Manohar Singh was declared the first
formal Chief of the Company and continued till I 985.
Over the years the Repertory has produced a variety of

plays ranging from stylised musicals, realistic
contemporary works of Indian theatre and also translation

and adaptations of foreign plays.
Various directors of India and abroad have worked with

the Company throughout the past years.
Apart from performing in Delhi the Company undertakes
extensive tours all over India and has also made
successful trips to G.D.R., Poland, Britian, Berlin and
Kadnnandu.

As part of the Silver Jubilee celebration the Company
took an extensive tour last year and further in the month
of April put up Rangyatra Theatre Festival and an

exclusive exhibition Designed by Shri Bansi Kaul
depicting the work of N.S.D. Repertory Company and its
cross section since inception through photographs,
models, Jewellery, costumes etc. This exhibition has been

on view in Delhi for over four months and has travelled to
Bombay, Pune, Goa and Bangalore.
