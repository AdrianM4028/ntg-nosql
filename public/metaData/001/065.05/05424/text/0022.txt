I agree that the audience is
of tremendous importance, I
suspect that there can be very
little greatness in the theatre
without what Whitman called
But the
‘Great audiences’.
great audience is not one that
simply wants relief from tedium
or even relaxation after exertion. A great audience is not
even a collection of great men.
It is an assembly of fully human beings with something in
common, something relevant
to the occasion. Audiences at
concerts and dance recitals are
often a good deal better than
audiences at plays, for they have
come together not as people who
demand to be amused but as
people interested in music or
dance. Even this degree of
solidarity is rare enough. Lack
of community is a problem not
of our arts but of our whole
civilization.
———ERIC BENTLEY.
