In the milieu of Styles and Forms that pervade the present-day
theatre—starting from the traditional Sanskrit Drama and
coming down to the highly fashionable Brechtian Alienation
Concept—one deﬁnite principle to have emerged is that of
socio-economic-political consciousness/relevance.
The indefatigability of our theme and its inherent diversiﬁed
contradictions made us choose a Style and Form which would
enable us to present the maximum in minimal time. This we strive
to achieve through . an amalgamation of Cinematic Juxtapositioning and Brechtian Alienation. Via ‘ media the. Cinematique
we present Inter and Cross-Cut Scenes, based in different
locales, but blended together by their thematic totality, while
the Brechtian Alienation has enabled us- to present a horde of
characters with a minimum use of actors. . In the Cinematique

we resort to “on camera” 1 treatment V wherein the actors start
performing when “the camera is switched on”. Through Alienation and Pantomimic Enactment we also strive to achieve 'an
objective evaluation of the situation. A few of the scenes are
conceived chorally for the purpose of enhancing magnanimity,
while excessive use of music is representative of the main character’s sub-conscious desire to achieve a blended harmony.

In order to focus 4 the viewers’ attention on the thematic essential
there is an absence of Costumes, Properties and Setting (in
scenic appropriation) as the use of these would have been a
distraction.
Lastly, Samuel Beckett, the noted playwright once said—
‘The playwright must, in order . to be heard, impregnate his
message in the laugh which turns into a tear’.
——S. Vasudev
