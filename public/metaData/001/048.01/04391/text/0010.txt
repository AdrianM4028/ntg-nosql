Othello has been in my mind for many decades. I
started my career with the production of Othello in
1966 in English but this production, the way it was
expected to be mounted, was to be a joint effort of my
senior friend and well wisher "Giri Rajji" who is no
more with us and was a great Nautanki singer and a
guru. He had acted in a student production of mine
more than twenty years ago. I found him a wonderful
actor who would also get out of the mould of the
Nautanki presentational acting style and would
himself to the new format of presentation.
We felt that of all the four tragedys this was one, in
which the certain elements of nautanki would blend
in nicely with the theme of Othello, specially because
of the martial atmosphere in the background. I felt
committed to doing the production in the memory of

this great artist, who is no more with us. In this play,
we are not doing it in the nautanki style. It would not
been in that style even if Giri Rajji were alive because
we had decided that we would use the elements of
music appropriate to the dramatic demand of the

scenes.
