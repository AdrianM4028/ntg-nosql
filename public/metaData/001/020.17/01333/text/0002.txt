ABOUT THE PLAY

३

To live life which has both meaning and creative energy is not only our foremost neccessity but also the
' biggest challenge. It seems almost inevitable in the prevailing social structure that a person in search of an
' identity must face endless pitfalls and frustrations. An individual’s identity and ﬂavour is often lost under
the burden ofrole responsibilities, expectations and duties thrust upon by the family, the socio-cultural setup and society. It is as if our society has been created solely for the sake of living in the moulds created by
‘others’. Where do we get time and space when we can take initiative to bring out the individual selffrom
within? The strength to take on the responsibility for one’s ownself gets blunted by the onerous demands
of societal and familial acceptance. Under the circumstances it is quite understandable for mental, spiritual
and political uncertainty (directionlessness) to grow and develop.
Kanti is also caught between the claustrophobic four walls ofsociety and need of space for his own self-ex—
pression. His mother, elder brother, Roshanlal, his friends Nitin and Asmita, all of them are trying to encircle and bind him within their own world-views. Will, Kanti be able to free himself of these bonds? Will
he be able to touch and reach his naked self after taking off the layers of social roles?
Well, the journey of search for finding this truth in its purity is on and will go on .... . .
