About the Director
Uday Narayana Shahane has been involved
in theatre for many years, during which time he
attended many workshops by eminent
directors like B.V. Karant, Bansi Kaul, Habib
Tanvir and MK. Raina. He has acted in more
than (33 plays, including ballets like the
Mahabharata and the Panc/Ifanfra; has
translated and adapted plays; has participated
in several festivals in India and abroad, and is
the recipient of numerous awards.

Rang Vidushak, established in 1984, is a team
of educated theatre enthusiasts who aim at
exploring new directions in performance.
Since its inception it has attracted many
performers from all walks of life. The focus of
the group is on children's theatre, training,
production, musical programmes, etc. It
continues its efforts to mould a theatre of
vidushaks within the context of the
contemporary dynamics of performance
traditions and to help in the innovation of a new
theatre language of laughter. The group has
conducted several theatre workshops in many
parts of the country and has participated in
numerous theatre festivals in India and abroad.
Some of its famous productions are Jadu
Ja/IgaI, Bed/fab Thandedaar, Gad/Ion Ka Me/a,
Saudagar, and many more .

Momma Play
Based on Aasha Bhajekar's short story 'Aala
Tareh Gela', the play is about a little girl, Deepu,
whose father abandons her another woman. One
day Deepu falls ill. She doesn't want to stay
alone but her mother has to go to work. Just
then a thief enters the house. Deepu mistakes
him for her father, and the thief, eager to steal
as much as possible without obstruction, also
pretends to be him. He plays with her, feeds
her, and finally takes the keys to the cupboard
from her. Deepu falls asleep and the thief steals
everything. But just as he is about to leave his
conscience rebukes him. Filled with affection
and sorrow for the little child sleeping in
innocence, he leaves without taking anything.
