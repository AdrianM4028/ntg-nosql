recognized essentially as an author of ﬁction. Born in Amritsar (Punjab) in a conservative
household, e completed postgraduate degrees in Sanskrit and Hindi, and started writing short
stories at an early age. He edited a literary journal, Sarika, for some time and, after a few stints at
teaching, took up freelancing as a profession because by temperament he was a rebel and
wanderer.

Through his small body of playsAshadh ka ek din (one day in Ashadha, published 1958), Lahron ke

House), and the unfinished Pair tale kin zarnin (“Ground Beneath the Feet')he brought a completely
new sensibility to the realistic genere. Each of his major works he set in a different historical frame,
milieu and class, to challenge himself to create a separate idiom rhythm, and arnbience His most

important contributions were the new awareness of and parameters for dramatic speech that he
evolved. He also achieved a remarkable crispness and theatricality in his writing through a
conscious effort to collaborate closely with eminent actors and directors, revising scripts
according to their observations in order to grasp the nuances of the perforrnatory language.
