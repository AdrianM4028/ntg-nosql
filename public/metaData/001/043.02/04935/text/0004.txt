Presenting Shakunta/a
If tradition means more the presence than the pastness of past, Abhijnanashakuntalarn
embodies that kind of tradition Intellectuals look for contemporary signiﬁcance in monuments of
unaging intellect. But that hardly matters. TWO events like the outrage against Dalit Women in
Maharastra and Delhi Chief Minister getting honorary Doctorate along with the Superstar Amitabh
Bacchan are no doubt contemporary to each other: But they are not present to each other. Classics
are always present and keep teasing us to go back to the Shakuntalarn is present to us in this sense
even after centuries.
For the play explores the perennial conflicts and reconciliation between male /female polarity with
all its symbolic dimensions. Man the hunter and women the protector's is the theme of recurrent
and variegated similes through out the play. The play does not resolve this elemental quarrel for or
against either of the parties. The reconciliation takes place from the angle of all embarrassing
compassion, which has become unfashionable in our era of recurring cycles of human idiocies.
Another important dimension of the play is the pattern of forgetting and remembering. The play
depicts this pattern by re—membering both the willful and subconscious acts of forgetting and
remembering. This is what is not found in the obituaries of the present or in the monuments of the
dead .

Such things keep Shakuntala still alive, present and inspiring fresh life into our own world
fragmented into discreet moments of consumer acts willed by forces we think beyond our
control .
