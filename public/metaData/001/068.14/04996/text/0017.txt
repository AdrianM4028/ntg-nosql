’I‘he Pala or Ballad, written in the 16th to 18th centuries, from an important folk genre.
Some of the most well known ballads of Bengal are the MAIMANSINGHA GEE’I‘IKA and
PURBA BANGA GEE’I‘IKA. The political social economic religious and cultural history of
Bengal is found in these Ballads. One of the most popular Ballad is the Chandrabati.

Chandrabati is a simple tale of love and languishment. Chandrabati meets Jayananda in
the garden where Chandra goes to pluck ﬂowers every morning and a friendship develops
amogst the two from their childhood. The friendship of childhood transforms into love of
young age. Chandra’s marriage is settled with Jayananda. But one day Jayananda meets
Ashmani the daughter of a Muslim Kaji and gets impressed by her beauty. Jayananda writes

love letters to Ashmani. Ashmani on knowing about Jayananda’s engagement with Chandra
shows the letters to the Kaji who forces Jayananda to marry Asmani. Chandra gets shocked
and refuses any further proposal of getting married. Instead she decides to take shelter in a
Shiv Mandir and Spend her time in worshipping. Jayananda, terribly repentant returns to

Chandra but the religios dogma dominates the normal humane passions and leads to a
tragic end.
