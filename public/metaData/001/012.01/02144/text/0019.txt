‘Chandrama Singh Urfa Chamkoo’ is a drama adaption of

writer LUXUN
AHQ is a representative character of a revolutionary Chi-

nese society. It is due to this character that the story writer gets an
opportunity to depict the attitude of such persons who either oppose the revolutionaries or exploit the common man.
AHQ of LUXUN is Chamkoo or Chandrarna Singh of this
play. Bhanu Bharti has depicted this eccentric character alongwith
all the details of his environment very skillfully. The play unveils
the causes of the massacre of the thousands of innocent youths
during Naksal Bari farmer revolution. Chandrama Singh Urfu
Chamkoo is massacred by the police because Chamkoo is having sympathy for those common men who have revolted against
the Land Lords, hoarders and exploiters.
“Chamkoo” directed by Bhanu Bharti was sponsored by
National School of Drama at Rangmanch-numa-gaon (Theatrical Village) in the year 1982. The success of the play can be
assessed by the fact that it left an indelible mark in the minds of
the audience. In fact this play has successfully portrayed the the—
matic connotations of LUXUN in typical regional style of Eastern
Uttar Pradesh.
