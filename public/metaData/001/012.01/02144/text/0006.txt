बर्वे

Begum Barve is a modern classic of the Marathi theatre.
Written origunally in 1993 by Satish Alekar, the play is a moving
tale of an old actor, Barve, who is a character drawn from the

Sangeet tradition. Barve, who played female roles, but never got
the opportunity to play the central roles, reminiscences the days
of Bal Gandharva. Unable to cope with his poverty, he now laments his past, and finds escape in fantasy, which are so vivid
and intense, that he lives them to a point of reality.
The plot delves the subconscious of Barve’s divided self,
living the past/present, as well as the male/female persona. The
play skilfully weaves the background and richness of the Marathi
Sangeet into a piece of modern writing, giving it layers of different textures of space and time.
