Prof. S. Ramajujam started his theatre career at a very young
age, and his association with E. Alkazi and (3. Sankara Pillai
proved to be a real turning point in his life. The Gandhigram
Rural Institute and its neighboring villagers — who were rich
in folklore — provided him an in-depth knowledge of the
emerging Indian theatre.
Prof. Ramanujam's various theatrical experiments with Prof.
G. Sankara Pillai in T'arnilnadu and Kerala gradually became
the springboard of several new initiatives. His stint as
Assistant Director of the School of Drama, T'richur, and later
as Professor and Head, Department of Drama, Tamil
University, Thanjavur, brought Prof. Ramanujarn to the
centerstage of contemporary Indian Theatre.
His passionate involvement in the development of healthy
theatre practice and experiments on theatre for both children
and adults made him popular among the theatre enthusiasts
in Kerala and Tamilnadu.

As a theatre person, I had the privilege to associate myself
with the playwright, Prof. Gi. Sankara Pillai in directing many
of his adult and children's plays. Besides being his colleague
for over five decades, I ﬁrmly believe that Sankara Pillai never
compromised on the principles governing the medium he
chose to work with. According to him, children's drama has
been a powerful medium of education and value inculcation
through entertainment and creative expressions by children.
Pillai's "Ponnumkudam" has been my favourite children's play
and has been presented in Tamil and Malayalam three times
in the past. Each presentation offered me insights and artistic
experiences which is quite different from one another: The
present design is the result of my growing realisation that the
play is about social criticism, based on live experiences of
people both in the higher echelons or strata and from streets
and slums. The social criticism rises to the level of sending
strong messages against social discrimination on the basis of
class, colour and caste. The interdependence of all human
beings and animals, besides highlighting all forms of life, has
also been highlighted subtly by the playwright. I tried my
utmost effort to weave into the total artistic frame of the
production. I have also heavily drawn on the creative talents
and experience of the Rangaprabhath Children's Theatre
Group in this transcreation.
