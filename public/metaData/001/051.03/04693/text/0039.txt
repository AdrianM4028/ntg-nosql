BA (The Breezee), a cultural organsation of Guwahati, Assam
was established in 1995 with a group of active theatre
workers. Artistic director Anup Hazarika heads the group. So
far the group has staged five major plays. He has directed all
of them. The plays include Agnigarh written by Arun Sarrna,
Menaka written by Jimani Chaudhury and Pakeezah Begum,
Siasa Paaneer Mass written by Mahendra Borthaxur,
Charandas Chor written by Habib Tanveer, and Rupaleen
written by Jyoti Prasad Agarwal, and Moi Tuniye Tuntunalo,
written by Nabakanta Boruah.

The play is based on a folk tale of Assam. It is about a rich
king who is proud of his wealth and property. He possesses a
huge quantity of money (unused silver coins) in his treasury.
One day a bird comes and steals one of the coins from his
treasury. The king gets angry and arrests the bird to take
revenge for her rnisdoings. He tries to punish the bird, but the
queens come to her rescue with the ignorance of king. Finally,
the king is being taught a good lesson by the bird for his
foolishness and misery.
Thus, the play revolves round with the king, queens, and his
courtiers having a great sense of humour, and educates the
children how to learn the act of being decent and merciful to
others.
