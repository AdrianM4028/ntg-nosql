The group was instituted with the sole objectives to undertake
the cultural programmes in and outside the state of Manipur. A
display of drama, music and street plays on important issues
and national theme is an integrated approach of its members.
Preservation of cultural heritage and solving socio-ethnic
problems in modern days is also one of the main activities of
the group. The members are well informed of the day-to—day
problems such as ethnic, communal riots, atrocities on women,
terrorism, bonded labour, imbalance of echo system etc. It has
full-fledged repertory that works in promoting theatre
movement to bring about a change in the years to come.
The repertory is an associate of the Theatre Centre, Manipur
an apex organisation of the theatre movement in Manipur that
coordinates in producing number of creative art projects. It has
the panel of twenty talented young artistes. Some of them have
already received scholarship from the Department of Culture,
Government of India. Besides, P Natek Singh, an eminent

emeritus fellow is also involved in motivating the young artistes
with a perspective view.
Recognising its credit in theatre, the Sangeet Natak Academy,
New Delhi has been supporting production grant since its
inception on recurring basis. Since its establishment, 30 plays
have already been staged so far at different parts of the
country including Inter-State Cultural Exchange Programmes.
The repertory also stages plays for Doordarshan Kendra,
Imphal, Programme Production Centre, Guwahati and AIR,
Irnphal. It has one of the research units in studying Folk
Theatre Festival Forms under the sponsorship of Ford
Foundation (USA) through Forum For Laboratory Theatre,

Eikhoisu Jibani Thawai Paliba, is a play based on the folk story
of Manipur. It is about an old childless couple living peacefully
in a remote village. They have a cow and a newly born calf,
namely Thoibi. They used to look after them like their own
children. The old man takes out the cow and calf on the
grassy field to graze them.
In the mean time, the two wicked persons - Kullabi
