Anup Hazarika, a graduate from National School of Drama,
was born in 1964 at Nitaipukhuri, Sibsagar in Assam. He has
been associated with Assamese amateur theatre, TV and film
industry. For a long time, he also gained a vast experience in
the mobile theatre industry of Assam. So far, he has directed
eighteen stage-plays in both amateur and mobile stage, and
participated in nearly 50 plays as an actor. To add to his
credit, he has also acted in eight Assamese feature films,
fifteen TV serials and ten short films. In 1995, he has
established his own group BA (The Breeze) at Guwahati. At
present, he is working as a lecturer in acting at Jyotichitralaya
Film & Television Institute, Kahilipara, Guwahati.

In July 2002, I was asked to direct a play in a children-theatre
workshop, organised by the Directorate of Cultural Affairs of
Assam in collaboration with National School of Drama. Moi
Tuniye Tuntunalo, written by Nabakanta Boruah became an
automatic choice as the play really meant for the children's
production, for the poetical dialogues of the play easily attract
the children. We have staged this play at Rabindra Bhawan,
Guwahati on July 30, 2002 in the concluding ceremony of the
workshop where 30 children were participated. We are trying
to tell the story of the play through the folk dance of Assam,

In the midway of our rehearsal process, the sad news came
that our beloved Boruahji, the playwright of this play passed
away. Mr Boruah, popularly known among the children as
Ekhud Kakaidema, who was supposed to interact with the
children in the workshop, was no more to entertain them. Our
children have already lost their bosom friend who had a soft
heart for them always. This play is a tribute to that great soul
that departed from us.
