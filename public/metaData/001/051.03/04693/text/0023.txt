Theatre-in-Education India Trust is a new entity promoted by
some experienced theatre persons working for theatre in
education, namely Vibha Chhibber and Walter Peter. It is totally
dedicated to the children, youth and teachers of the country. The
group‘s objective is to work for and with the children to provide a
friendly environment among the teachers, parents and
themselves through theatre as a medium of education.
It creates a space — both mental and physical where all these
children shape up a good career in future education. It has the
potential to work wonderfully with them and help solve their
complicated questions on any particular issues. It also serves as
a forum for sharing the natural curiosity that springs from deep
inside. It leads to increasing curiosity and concentrations,
developing the co—operative spirit and art of living together,
learning to accept responsibility, to take on challenges, to fight
hard, to head a team, to learn to speak and listen, see their faith
getting a boost and so on. So far, the group has been involved in
doing a whole lot of workshops with the children, teachers and
teachers' trainees with the help of the organisations like
Sarnbhav, Jaipur, Anand Niketan School, Ahmedabad, Bhartiya
Vidya Bhawan, Bhopal, Shikshantar, Udaipur, Mobile Creches and
Manzil and SCERT, Delhi. It is also one of the members of

'Learning Society', which have an International membership.

The play revolves round with two teenagers — Tanu, Manu,
whose parents ignore them always. Parents' lack of time
causes a great concern for the children's future, for now
they are burdened with the pressure of their examinations
this year. In this play, many a question arises — Whom can
they talk with? With their friends or with you? Or should
they remain content by themselves?
