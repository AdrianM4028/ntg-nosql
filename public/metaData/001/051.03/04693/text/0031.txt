Alarippu, (means blossoming), was established in
1983. The group's activities have been spread all
over the country. Generally, it has been focusing on
amateur theatre, youth, women and folk theatre.
Since 1988 the group has been working with
children too, in and out of schools. It believes that
creativity is not only inherent in every child or
individual but it is also one of the ways to expand
and express one's self and experience. It tries to
create the space and atmosphere, which makes an
exploration possible. While working with the
children, Alarippu has experimented with creative
drama, poetry, songs, art and clay-work,
photography and story making, writing and telling.
Theatre workshops for the children and teachers
have been an important activity of the group. One
of the children's productions — Gadariya Aur Raja
by Tripurari Sharma — has been effectively
performed in schools of Delhi. Connecting with the
children either directly or indirectly through books
and mobile libraries has been a special and
gratifying experience for the group. Currently, the
group is involved in an ongoing education project
for young girls and boys in remote areas of Supa in
Ajmer District, Rajasthan.

The play starts with a narrator, who is acting as a child. He interacts
with the group children, searching for Gaurav. Meanwhile, Gaurav with
a bat in his hand joins the group. Then they start playing cricket.
Gaurav calls his sister, Gauri to join them. But she refuses. She seems
to be upset. Gaurav tries to make her laugh, but to no avail. Gaurav
then understands the reason what goes wrong with her, as the father
refuses her to gift a cycle. Since she has been denied a gift, he decides
to buy one for her.
When he has to take the money out of his savings box, his
