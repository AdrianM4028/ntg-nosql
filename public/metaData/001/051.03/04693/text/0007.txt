Kala Jagaran was established in 1990 in Patna. Various
theatre activists, intellectuals and scholars have been
associated with the group since its inception. Ganesh Prasad
Sinha and Surnan Kurnar are the core members of the group.
The group has already produced many street and stage plays,
audio-visuals and other cultural programmes. Babu Ji Ka Pass
Book written by Madhukar Singh and directed by Surnan
Kurnar, has been one of the popular productions of the
group. This play has also been performed more than 25 times 1
in different parts of Bihar. Besides, the group has produced
other plays like Jas Karni Tas Bhog, Swatantrata Ki Pukar,
Fas Gaya Bad/u Jai/ Mein, Siksha Hal Anmo/ Ratan etc.
Recently, it has prepared a couple of productions like Bedhab
Thanedar and Zarur'at Eshi Ki Thi.

The theme of the play is about the cruel system of child
labour in our society. It generally portrays the emotions of the
child in this vicious circle. In this play, Kalua is a servant of a
social worker, Chaturananda, who often beats up Kalua even
for his small mistakes.
In the name of social service, Chaturananda takes advantage
of not only Kalua, but many of the children and makes good
money out of this ‘business'. He kidnaps the children and sells
them off to petty businessmen who employ them in fireworks
and other factories.
A real social worker comes to know about the act of illtreating children by Chaturanand. He makes an effort to get
them free from Chaturananda's clutch with the help of police.
It generally talks about how a child destroys his/her childhood
through the interference of a group of antisocial elements,
who have a good links with corrupt politicians and
police. It
also depicts the inhuman circumstances faced by the
innocent
children, cruelty and torture meted out to them
by moneyhankering bosses in related industries.
