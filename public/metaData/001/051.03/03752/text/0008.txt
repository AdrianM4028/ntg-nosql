About the Play
A few years back, the city of Murnbai
(Bombay) was rocked by bomb blasts

& communal riots. The religious क्या
झुंदुड्डेहुँफुतु ending up with a huge loss of
human life and property. A lot of
innocents were injured and killed.
These riots further widened the existing cracks in the communal
hannony

The after-effects of the riots were also substantial. People started
worrying about security and survival. A feeling of suspicion and
mistrust was formed between the members of the communities.
A telling example of this was when parents started shifting their
children from the cosmopolitan schools to the "safer" schools having
a communal identity. The linguistic identity. The linguistic identity
started becoming religious identity.
The play " Pan Amhala Khelaychay" deals with this subject. Children
from the cosmopolitan school resist their separation on religious
grounds. They have not lost the trust in each other though the elders
have. A near translation of the title is "But We want to play together"
(Par Hamen Khelna Hai). This is children's reaction to the adult

plans of separating them.
