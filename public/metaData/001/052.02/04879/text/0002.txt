The training in the School is based on a thorough,
comprehensive, carefully planned syllabus that covers
every aspect of theatre in which theory is related to
practice, and in which all work is ultimately put to test
before a wide public audience. The syllabus takes into
account the methods of great theatre personalities who
have shaped contemporary theatre in all its various forms
of expression. The systematic study and practical
performing experience of Sanskrit drama, modern lndian
drama, traditional Indian theatre forms, Asian drama and
Western drama give the students a solid grounding and a
wide perspective in the art of theatre.

The School has a Repertory Company and a Theatre—in—
Education Company in Delhi and a Regional Resource
Centre at Bang lore. NSD's Extension programme seeks to
extend and diffuse its activities in al | regions of the country.
A programme for research, documentation and publication
is a recent addition to strengthen the academic activities of
the School. The School's publications include anthologies
of plays in different lndian languages translated into
English, reference works and theatre studies in Hindi and
two regular periodicals, viz. 'Rang Prasang' in Hindi, and
'Theatre India in English.

Each year NSD organizes an Annual Theatre Festival called
the Bharat Rang Mahotsava, and two bi—annual festivals
called Jashnebachpan and Bal Sangarn.
