About the Plav
The Caucasian Cha/k Ciro/e begins with a
Prologue that deals with a dispute over a valley
and is actually about two stories that come
together in the end It is Brecht‘s classic tale of
Grusha, the servant girl and her struggle to
tend and protect the governor's abandoned
child in a war—torn country. It culminates with
the final test of her motherhood when she is
torn between winning her child at the cost of
inflicting pain on it or losing it forever.
