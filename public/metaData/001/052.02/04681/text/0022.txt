Langa : The Langa community hails from the Western part
of Rajasthan, mainly from the districts of Jaiselmer and
Barmer in North West India. A rich village culture developed
in the vast unfriendly deserts inspired by long nights under the
stars. Though Muslims by birth they are closely linked since
generations to both Muslim and Hindu families of patrons
calledJajmaans. Whatever be the occasion, at the house of a
Jajmaan, the Langas are there with the appropriate song and
music. During annual festivals and festivities related to rites of
passage like engagements, marriages, births, and even the
sorrow of separation from a beloved, these professional artists
are called upon to sing and play in homes and temples, and
are remunerated for their services by their patrons in different
ways. The music of the Langas borders on the classical, as
much in their mastery in singing, as in their virtuosity in
playing various instruments: The Sarangi, the Murli (big ﬂute),
the Surnai (big bass ﬂute), the Algoza (double ﬂute), the
Morchhang (Jewish harp) and the Khartaal (type of castanets).
