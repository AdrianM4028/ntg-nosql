"Bhand" is a community that used to sing and dance on
the occasions like marriages. Later "Pather" (play) was
included as an integral part of "Bhand" and thus the
form "Bhand Pather" came into existence.
It is mostly suggestiye in nature and secular in outlook.
It has always been a satire on the contemporary social
problems.

Kashmiri folk tale of a "Gosaine" (Sadhu) and a

milkmaid Gopali, who falls in love with him. The
Gosaine, unaware of her infatuation, takes her keen

interest to be a desire for spiritual enlightenment. One
day when she expresses her love for him, the Gosaine
unable to face her beauty, vanishes.

The play does not end here for the dilemma now is
how to perform last rites in the absence of the body A
situation open to satire which is taken up by the three
Vidushakas in the right spirit of the folk form with
much aplornb.
