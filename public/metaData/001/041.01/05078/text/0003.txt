Shama Futehally
An M.A. ( 1 st Class) with English and Aesthetics from Murnbai University,
Shama Futehally was born in Mumbai in l 952. After serving as Lecturer in
English, Rarnnarain Ruia College, Mumbai, and Visiting Lecturer, (Post-

Graduate) Murnbai University, she was awarded a British Council
Scholarship to pursue M .A. in English from University ofLeeds ( 1978—79).
She was a visiting Lecturer in Humanities at the School of Architecture,

Ahmedabad and for World Drama at the National School of Drama, New
Delhi during 1993- 1996. While working as an Associate Professor of
World Drama in the National School of Drama, she had to her credit the
following publications: Tara Lane, a novel published by Ravi Dayal
Publisher, New Delhi (1993); In the Dark of the Heart: Songs of Meera
(1993), a translation of Bhajans by Meerabai in English (for which she was
also awarded funding from the Arts Council of Great Britain) published by
Harper Collins International, the Indian edition- published by Harper
Collins India (1996); A translation of Qaid—e-Hayat, an Urdu play on
Ghalib by Surendra Verma. Published by NSD, New Delhi, (2000);
Reaching Bombay Central a novel published by Viking, Penguin India,
(2002) and Short stories that were published in the following anthologies:
The Inner Courtyard (Virago Press, London, 1990), In Other Words
(Kali for Women, New Delhi, 1992), Gateway to India (Forlegat Systime,
Denmark, 1 9 9 2 ) , A
