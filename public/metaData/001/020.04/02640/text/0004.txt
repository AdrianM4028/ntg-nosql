'Asmita' stands committed to aesthetically innovative and socially revlevant
theatre. It takes up contemporary issues to underline the contours of our
time while providing the best of entertainment. So far the group has
staged Bhisharn Sahni's "Hanoosh" (26 shows), Albert Camus’
'Caligula' (21 shows), Raghuvir Chaudhary's 'Sikandra Sani (one for
Urdu Academy Festival-93), Dr. Girish Karnad's "Tughlaq' (47 shows),
' Rakt-Kalyan' (31 shows), Dr. Dhararnvir Bharti's 'Andhayug' (9 shows),
Brecht's Exception & the ru|e(street play) Shakespeare's 'Julius Caeser'
(8 shows) & Merchant of Venice (as 'Dilfarosh' for Urdu Academy's
festival '96), Eugene O'Neil's Desire under the Elms (14 shows),
Swadesh Deepak’s Court-martial (41 shows), Narendra Mohan's
Qalander (Urdu Academy's Festival 1997) and Dario Fo's Operation
Three Star (21 shows). ,In support of people's struggle against
victimisation, "Asmita" gave street-shows of Safdar Hashmi's Aurat (34
times) and Chicago 1886 (36 times), Saiah Jahan (21 shows). Mahesh
Dattani's Final solutions (9 shows) Tasveer (17 shows) All under the
direction of Arvind Gaur.
As an umbrella organisation to healthy theatre activities, "Asmita"
organised workshops at different colleges in Delhi which led to the
production of Brecht's The Good Person of Setzuana at l.P. College,
Bhisharn Sahni's Madhavi at Gargi College and Dario Fo's Accidental
Death of an Anarchist at School of Planning & Architecture. The group
organised the two-and-half hour long solo performance of Piyush Mishra
which enthralled the audience at Jaipur and Calcutta also.

In recognition of its endeavours, the group has been invited to perform
Court-martial as the only entry in Hindi at National Drama Festival at
Calicut (Kerala). 'Asmita' has also performed in Assam and Jammu &
Kashmir.
