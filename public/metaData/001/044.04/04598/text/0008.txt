३३ 1 Shas had har Acha rya
Representing the fifth generation in a family of traditional
performers who have significantly contributed in the evolution
and development of this art form, Shashadhar Acharya is
steeped in the tradition of Seraikella Chhau. He has inherited
the knowledge of this art in legacy from his father, Guru
Lingaraj Acharya.
Though he is creative in his methods of presentations he
always aspires to be true to the traditions that he has inherited.
A deep-rooted knowledge of his art gives him the confidence to
use it in collaborative works with dancers, actors and directors
of both traditional and modern disciplines. He has been
performing in several national and international festivals like Yuva Mahotsava organized
by Sahitya Kala Parishad, New Delhi; Festivals of Chhau, Organized by the Indian
Council for Cultural Relations, New Delhi; Festivals of India in USSR sponsored by the
Indian council for cultural relations; Festivals of India in Germany sponsored by the
Indian council for cultural relations etc. He has been associated as a movement teacher
with National School of Drama, New Delhi, Sriram Centre for Arts and Culture, New
Delhi; Theatre Works, Singapore and so on.

For his sustained and innovative work in Seraikella Chhau, Shri Shashadar Acharya has
been awarded the Sangeet Natak Akadern i Award in 2004.
