For the dramatic presentation of the play I have worked in
various dramatic possibilities always bearing in mind Relevant
Traditional Norms of dramatic folk forms, painting, music
(singing and narrative styles), puppetry, masks, clowns etc. i
always inspire mask and clowns dances of Goa’s famous
Carnival, CHITRAKATHI of Maharashtra, Pabujiki-Pad of

Rajasthan and stylize TURKISH miniatures & Chitrarath
procession of Shigmotsav. These motives could sustain the
fantasy of the play with stylisation by actors with the puppet,
mask, acrobatics, mime and art of story telling.

The contemporary relevance of the play is its satire on the
accidental achievement of high commanding status without
any work or deserving that merit a common phenomenon of

our time. The other factor of contemporary relevance would
be in satisfying the artistic and aesthetic need of present day

Indian theatre to find logical and artistically excellent norms in
the indigenous tradition.
