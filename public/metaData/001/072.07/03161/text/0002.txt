'Andhere Mein' is a nightmare come alive. Gajanana Madhav
Muktibodh wrote this rather longish poem when Indian
democracy was in its in fancy. Earlier, Muktibodh had written a
book, "India : its history and culture" Which got the goat of

the RSS & the then Jansangh (the forerunners of the present
BJP). The Sangh Parivar raised a hue and cry and managed to
get the book banned. This incident shocked Muktibodh deeply.
He began to perceive the authoritarian and fascist tendencies
latent in a democracy. "Andhere Mein" is an outcome of his
brush with reality.
Forty years have since gone by. These years have been witness
to brutal suppression of human rights and tragic killings of Safdar
Hashmis, Shankar Guha Niyogis and Chandrashekhars who
dared to raise their voice in deference of people‘s rights. There
have been concerted efforts to stifle cultural and artistic freedom
in the recent past. All these depressing scenarios only
corroborate what Muktibodh had anticipated in 'Andhere Mein'
four decades ago.

लेकिन ऐसे लोगों के बिना भारतीय लोकतंत्र का बचाए रखना असंभव होगा।

But this is not to say that 'Andhere Mein' is a poem only about
the onset of fascist tendencies in Indian democracy. It is also a
poem that explores the anxieties of the lower middle class,
especially the intelligentsia with regard to its social
responsibilities. All these socially conscious people realise that
their indifference has given impetus to authoritarian traits. Who
are those people, who can carry the society forward? Can the
middle class identify itself with such people '?
