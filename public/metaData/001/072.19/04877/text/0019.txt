BE तै COMPLETE HCTOR
Many people think that Drama training is a waste of time. "Act—
ing is something you can either do or can't do, how can you teach someone to do something that comes naturally or not at all", they ask ।
But we all are performers. We do perform from time-to—time.
Acting resides in everyone, it is an early an instinctive means of expression. It is how we learn to live by imitation. and yet acting is not merely
imitation, many actors cannot "mimic" at all. If acting were simply selfexpression or an amusing way of keeping peoples attention there would
be no need to train. Perhaps communication with an audience cannot
be taught to someone who has no innate ability Drama schools and .
Institutes should be a bridge between Professionals and Amateur act.
ing groups. Degrees. honours, diplomas are of no avail because every

time you act you have to prove you can do it ।
It is true-man is a born actor. Every one can act but all of us don't try to act ! Acting is a 'Sport' On
stage you must be ready to move like a tennis player on his toes. Your cancentration must be keen , your
reflexes sharp (some thing might go wrong). Your body and mind in top gear, the chase is on ।
Acting is energy ! In the theatre. people pay to see the energy. Actors are not against the
audience,

but with each other to fall them. They trick them, keep them in suspense, play with them. To act,
you
