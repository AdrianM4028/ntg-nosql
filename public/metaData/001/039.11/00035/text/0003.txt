The Playwright

Betsuyaku Minoru was born in
Manchuria in 1937. He dropped
out of the Politics-economics

department of Waseda university in
1960. He was one of the founders
of ‘Jiyu Butai’ (The First Stage), the

group which later become famous
as the “Waseda Little Theatre”.
In 1968 Betsuyaku won the “Kishida
Prize” for playwriting for two

plays: “The Little Match Girl”
(Matchi-uri no shojo) and “Where
Red Birds Dwell” (Akai Tori no
iru Fukei)
