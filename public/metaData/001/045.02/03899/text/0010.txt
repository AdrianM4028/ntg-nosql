Tennessee Williams (Thomas Lanier Williams) was born on March 26, 191 1 in
Columbus, Mississippi. He spent most ofhis youth in St. Louis, Missouri. After

ﬁlmed in 1951 and won Williams his ﬁrst Pulitzer Prize in drama, with the

Other successful plays by Williams are Summer and Smoke rewritten as
Eccentricities ofa Nightingale , The Rose Tattoo ( 1950) and Night ofthe Iguana
(1961). Although Williams continued to write for the theatre, he was unable to
repeat the success of his early works. One of his last plays was Clothes for a

Summer Hotel (1980), based on the relationship of American writer F. Scott
Fitzgerald and his wife, Zelda. Williams died in New York City on February 25,

1983.
