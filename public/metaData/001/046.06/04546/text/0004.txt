Born in 1940 in Darbhanga, Bihar, Shri Ram Gopal Bajaj took his Bachelor’s degree from Bihar
University in 1960 and then attended the National School of Drama, Delhi, specializing in acting.
He was one of the founder—members of Dishantar, a group of professional theatre workness established

in 1967. His performance in Suno Janmejaya, Hayavadan, Begarn Ka Takia, Ghasirarn Kotwal, Andha
Yug, Tughlaq and King Lear were high acclaimed. Later he established himself as director with the
production of Aashad Ka Ek Din, Surya Ki Antirn Kiran Se Surya Ki Pahli Kiran Tak, Qaide-e-Hayat,
Skandagupt, Muktadhara, Andha Yug and Charuvak, Notable among his production of non Indian plays are Maxim Gorky's Lower Depths
and Leo Tolstoy's The Power of Darkness. He has also produced about fifty plays for children when he served as a teacher for Drama
of Modern School, New Delhi. He has translated and adapted about eighteen plays including Girish Karnad's Aks Tarnasha, Chittaranjan
Ghosh’s Chittaranjan Ghosh’s Nati Binodini, Brecht's The Caucasian Chalk from 1967-71. Besides theatre, Ram Gopal Bajaj is well
known as an actor on radio, television and cinema. He is also known for his recitation of poetry.

He was Reader in the Deptt. of Theatre, Chandigarh in 1973 and Professor and Head of the Department of Dramatic Arts in Punjabi
University, Patiala during 1979-80. He has been the Chief of the NSD Repertory Company from 1988-94. He has been the director of
National School of Drama from 1996—2001.
He is a recipient of the Sahitya Kala Parishad (Delhi) Sarnrnan for Theatre direction (1992).
Shri Ram Gopal Bajaj received the Sangeet Natak Akaderni Award in 1996 for his contribution of Indian theatre. He was also honoured
by Nandikar, Calcutta in 1998. The Hyderabad University felicitated him by offering the prestigious Radha Krishanan chair. While in the
chair he directed Dhararnveer Bharati's 'Andha Yug‘ in Telugu. Recently he received the Padarnshree Award in 2003. Presently he is a

free-lance actor and director.
