In the contemporary theatre scene one does get
to know about Western plays and Greek tragedies
but the theatre worker of today is virtually ignorant
of his own tradition. In this light, experiments of this
kind are surely expected of NSD. Such experiments, when staged are usually done
keeping the prevalent conditions in mind, because
there is no definite living tradition of Sanskrit theatre, that is available. As such the director in such

experiments has to use his/her own style, which of
course cannot be objectively called the style of Sanskrit play production because it is bound to differ
from individual to individual.
If we try and approach the play from a realistic
viewpoint it will appear to be just a bundle of mixed
contradictions. The story line is also a pot—pourri of
folktales, events from the Puranas and legends. Even
if the story line is not probable it certainly is palatable.

It really was a challenging and difficult task choosing “Vikrarnorvashiyam” but the brilliancies and poetic genius of Kalidasa in portraying the extremes of
human love and passion doesent but attract one towards doing it. It simply is an analogy of his genius
that he has converted this web of different lores into
the Sangamaniya Mani (The magical gem that makes
estranged lovers come together) which infact is the
object that makes the reunion possible.
As one proceeds with an experiment of this kind
in theatre and goes deep into it, the depths appear to
become unfathomable.
Vikramorvashiyam is a kind of a fantasy, therefore an effort has been made to take the music to an
imaginative space and time.
