This play is about the love between the Chandrvanshi
emperor I’ururava and the beautiful fairy Urvashi.
By his valour Purvrava rescues Urvashi from the
hands of the demon Keshi. Their first meeting becomes a reason for their falling in love.

Urvashi first sends a love letter written on a
‘plam’-leaf due to which I’ururava comes to know
about her love. This letter coincedéntly falls into
the hands of the king’s first wife. But the queen
behaves in a thoughtful manner and sacrifices all her
interests in favour of her rival Urvashi; by the observance of a vow called I’riyanuprasadan. This gesture paves the way for a lasting union between
Urvashi and I’ururava.
Urvashi’s love for the king is found out by everybody when a slip of tongue gives her away. In a
play directed by Guru Bharat, Urvashi who is enacting the character of Laxmi utters “Pururva” instead
of saying “Purushottam Narayan”. Bharat Muni looses
his temper and banishes Urvashi from the heavens.
But Indra intervenes and converts the curse into a
boon that allows Urvashi to stay with I’ururava, till.
he sees his son.
The plot reaches a climax when I’ururava is
attracted towards a Vidyadhara girl. Blind with
jealousy and unforgiving by nature Urvashi enters
the forbidden Kumarvan and gets turned into a
creeper. Her absence leaves the king in utter
despair and he wanders in the forest in search
of her. A magical red gem sangamani makes
their reunion possible. The same gem becomes
reason for the unexpected meeting between
Pururava and his son Ayush. The meeting
reminds Urvashi of Bharata’s curse and it throws
everyone into gloom. Pururava decides to crown
his son and leave for the forest.
