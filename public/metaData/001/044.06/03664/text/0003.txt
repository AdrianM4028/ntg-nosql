Pala Eavan
Pala Gayan enriches culture. In other words, it represents a technique, a process and a tradition.
According to scholars Pala comes from Pali language. Pali means - praise of Lord — and since the
Pala actor invokes the Lord in the beginning - hence the name.
Pala is an amalgamation of rythrn, dance, body movement, lyricism, poetry, logic Puranas and

Ved-Upanishad etc.
Based on five different gods of religion the famous poet Kama wrote many Pala scripts in the

16th century.
Writings by Valmiki, Vyas, Bhas and Kalidas are also rendered in Pala Gayan. This also includes
writing & poetry of Hindi, Bengali & other languages which makes it a popular, peoples art giving it a

contemporary relevance.
Pal a Gayan has been acting as a means to secularism, composition & love for humanly in Orissa
since centuries!
