Nadira Zaheer Babbar is an established director, playwright,

actor and producer. Born into the illustrious family of Sayyed
Sajjad Zaheer, one of the founders of the Progressive Writers
Association, the Indian People's Theatre Association (lP'l'A)
and the Afro-Asian Writers’ Association, she is a graduate
from the lsobella Thoburn College in Lucknow and holds a
Diploma in Acting from the National School of Drama, New
Delhi. After her studies in India, she moved to East Germany,
traveled widely in the UK, Europe and the then Soviet Union
and worked with the Berliner Ensemble and eminent directors
like Jerzy Grotowski, Fritz Bennewitz, Wolfgang Heinz and
Henry Howard. On her return to India, she worked for some
years as a drama teacher in the Sardar Patel Vidyalaya in Delhi
and then dedicated herself full time to the theatre.
In 1981 she set up Ekjute, a theatre company with more than
50 members today. Ekjute has pioneered various forms of
theatre since its formation, set standards of excellence and
provided a platform to various artists, many of whom, like
Anupam and Kiron Kher, Anita Kanwar', Satish Kaushik, Kavita
Chaudhary and Raja Bundela, have since gained national
renown.
