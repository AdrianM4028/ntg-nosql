The Director
Roysten Abel graduated from NSD in 1994 and went to
apprentice with the Royal Shakespeare Company in the
same year. In 1999 he devised and directed Othello: A Play in

Black and White, which was his first original work and won
the Scotsman Fringe First Award. This work was followed by
a spate of original works which he conceived and directed,
some of them produced in India and some in Europe.
He then started to work with outofawork street performers
(magicians, jugglers, snake charrners, acrobats, puppeteers,
etc.), and was invited to conceive and direct a play on Fellini
in Rimini. His major productions include Spirit of Anne
Frank, The Manganivar Seduction, and A Hundred Charmers.
