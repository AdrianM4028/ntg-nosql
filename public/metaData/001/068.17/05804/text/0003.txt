DIRECTOR’S PLEA
Bertolt Brecht’s “The Good Person of Szechwan” has been a favourite of the
Indian Directors. Even Nandikar once did a Production of this Play and it had
earned quite a name. So why produce the same old Play — however new-look and
newly adapted -- once again : I could spin off umpteen reasons : e.g. it is healthy
for the theatre to even over—indulge in Classics; or, this Play allows you to
involve a great number of actors and the fun of multiple games for a multitude;

or again, you could atone for the ‘Consumerism' of such a useful play which
Nandikar did in 1974 under organisational and financial duress; or— simply, I
LOVE THIS PLAY and so on and so forth. But the reason which overwhelms me
to return to this Play is different : Ours is a time when the moral ground under
our feet is indeed slippery, when equations between Good and Evil are so tricky,
when human goodness is becoming an expendable item. The Good. Person_
challenges me to hazard a journey to goodness and it excites us to. the task.of 1

synthesisng the individual 3 with the Good of the other 3 And thatis for me the
challenge of our time.
