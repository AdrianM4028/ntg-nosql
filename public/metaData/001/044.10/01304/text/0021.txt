Translation

He who parts the curtain on yestarday.
Translation
You with the elephant head, the son of Maheswara. You

who rides the mouse and carries a ’laddoo’ on his palm.
You who carries the santimoru'us ’Pashan-kushdhara’.
You in the ’Vaman-rupa’ and with ’Shyamal-karna’.
Before you I prostrate to grant me a boon.

He who fills our tomarrow.
He who makes us dance today, and

He who designs the course of our future.
To him, Be praise and glory; praise and glory.
(The above stu ties in Kannada are sung just before opening of the play and in end
of the play respectively.)
