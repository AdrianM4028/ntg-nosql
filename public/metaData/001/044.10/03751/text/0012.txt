Director's Note
This production is evolved through a series of improvisations
by the studentsThe emphasis , throughout . had been on 'the
process“ than on the end product, as it is an exercise meant for
the students to learn, to design and perform Sanskrit
classics .We discovered in the process. how this ancient text is
surprisingly contemporary. This is the first public performance
of this batch of the students.
I am grateful to Ankur Ji , who introduced the text to the
students through his brilliant analysis of the play in his own
characteristic style , with wit and humour. I am grateful to Sh.
Dinesh Khanna and Dr. Ratna Panikkar for their valuable
contribution. SmtAnjana Rajan and ShLokesh Bharadwaj
trained the students in classical dance movementsl am

extremely grateful to them. My thanks are also due to
Dr. Balgovind Sagar who ensured proper rendering of Sanskrit
Shlokas and also Hemant Bhatnagar for designing the
poster/brochure.
Without the gurdance and support of HVS and Bhaskar
Chandavarkar. I would not have ever ventured to direct a
Sanskrit play. Bhaskarji always remains a source of inspiration
for us even though he could not join us in this production.
