P U N AR.l A N I
The world has been taken over by a cruel Dictator.
In his court are produced his vanquished foes-Niyamam
(Law), Sathyarn (Truth or Righteousness), Socialism. The
Dictator ridicules them in turn and challenges their existence and validity. Niyarnarn is asked where he was when
Christ was crucified and Socrates was given poison. Sathyam
is asked where he was when Gandhiji, the champion of
Truth and Righteousness, was assassinated. Socialism
is jeered at and told that he exists only for the selfish
interest of himself, his relatives and his sycophants. When
Kalarn comes with his vivacious light the Dictator hurls the
lamp away plunging the world in darkness.

Times change and with it Sathyarn (Truth) awakens.
Sathyarn releases Niyamarn and Socialism and altogether
they overpower the Dictator. The Young Generation, who
lives in utter disgust, arrives and asks their assistance for
getting out of the darkness of the world. Kalam extends
support and advises him to go ahead without fear.
