Kargam
Kargam is a folk dance of Tamil Nadu which originated as a ritual
dedicated to Mariamman the goddess of health and rain—she is also the
protector from the dreaded small pox and cholera. This ritual is performed
in August, idol of Mariarnman is carried in procession. The ritual pot filled
with water and adorned with decorations several feet high is carried by the
priest. The dancers perform various acrobatic feats while following the
procession accompanied by a number of musical insturments like ‘Thavil’,
“Nadaswaram”, “Muni”, “Udukkai”, “Parnbai” etc.
