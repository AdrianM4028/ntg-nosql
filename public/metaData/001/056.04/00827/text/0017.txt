K ud
This is a typical community dance performed in the middle mountain
ranges of Jammu. During the rainy season, when the maize is harvested,
the villagers come down from the nearby hills and gather in the vicinity of
the local deity the “gramdevta”. To express their gratitude for protecting
their crops, cattle and children from natural calamities, the farmers dance

“Kud” to the deity. Men, women and children, attired in their best, gather
around a bonfire for the nightlong festivities. The muscians, playing
“Ransingha”, “Bansaries”, “Talli” and “Dhauns” enter the circle first and
are followed by the other dancers in single file.
The costume of the dancers vary from place to place as do the songs sung
with the “Kud” dance often performed all night on moonlit nights.
In the Bhadarwah area, songs are in the “Bhadlai” dialect while, in the
“Ladhe Di Dhar” area of Udhampur district they are in pure Dogri.

“Kud” means a fair or rnela and was danced at fairs. But it is now a
common dance for all festive occasions including celebrations of a
marriage, the birth of a child etc. The pilgrims while going on ”Kailash
yatra” from Bhadarwah also dance “Kud”.
