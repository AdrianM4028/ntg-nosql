Diwali Dance
Diwali assumes great significance for the dancers of Bundelkhand. The
Ahir (milkmen) community in the district of Hamirpur, Mahoba and
Banda of Uttar Pradesh, perform Diwali Nritya or Stick Dance. Its name
denotes its martial character, the dance is performed by males who move in
circles holding long sticks in their hands with ‘ghoongrus’ on their feet and
waist to provide the rhythm for the fast movements of the dance which
depicts the gestures of attacking and protecting simultaneously. The
performers wear knickers studded with tiny bells and as they dance, they
rhythmically strike sticks held in their hands. Sitting and skipping
positions are also assumed.
