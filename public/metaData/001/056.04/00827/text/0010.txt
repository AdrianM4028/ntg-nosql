Chham Chhank
The Lama’s of Lahul Spiti region of Himachal Pradesh perform this
devotional dance related to their well known ‘Chakhar’ festival which
comes after every three years to pay tribute to Mahatarna Budha.
They wear a typical head gear known as ‘Chhank’ thus the name
‘Chham Chhank’. The costume made with costly golden, black and yellow
cloth decorated with traditional motifs in patch work, is very distinctive.
Some dancers carry swords and others knives while covering half their
faces with a peice of black cloth.

This is a very slow dance with circular movement and accompanied by
musical instruments like ‘Tangchirn’ — the long pipes and ‘dhol’ like
‘than’. The musicians wear the traditional lama ‘libas’ alongwith
beautiful caps decorated with yellow feathers etc.
