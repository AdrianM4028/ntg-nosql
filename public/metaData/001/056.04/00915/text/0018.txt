Chholiya

This is one of the most popular folk dances of Mizoram. The Mizos
brought it with them from their early abode in the far East. It is a
community dance. It is also commonly known as the bamboo dance.
Actually it has no connection with the bamboo. In earlier days, the
dancers usually danced over dressed wooden sticks. There is no
particular occasion for performing Cheraw, it is for entertainment and
merry making.

Prevalent in the Kumaon region of Uttar Pradesh, the Chholiya dance
has elements of martial craft and is associated with the Kirji Kumbh
celebrations. Kirji Kumbh is a poisonous flower which blossoms every
12 years. Villagers march in a procession to destroy the ﬂower before it
sheds its poison into the mountain streams. Both men and women
participate in the dance wearing their distinctive costumes.
