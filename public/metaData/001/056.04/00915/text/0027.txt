Lahoo

Lebang Bomani

The Lahoo dance is one of the traditional dances of the Jaintia tribe of
Meghalaya. In fact the original name of the dance is the Chipiah dance.
Presumably. the name Lahoo has origins in the rythrnic chant of the
singer to the dancing tune. ‘hoo-ah-hoo‘. This particular dance is
performed by man to show his gratitude to God’s bounteous blessings.
This dance is regarded as the best token of man’s expression of gratitude
to God‘s mercy and love.

Lebang Bomani is a fascinating harvest dance of Tripura. The dancers
play bamboo clappers with a drone that attracts grasshoppers which are
collected by the dancers. It is believed that the more grasshoppers they
catch the more will be the yield of the crops.

Performed by R. Masser and team members. Shillong, Meghalaya

The dance is performed both by the men and women. The men clap
the ‘tokkas’ or bamboo clappers while the women join the circle waving
colourful scarves.

The accompanying instruments are Sarinala, a bow string and a drum
called Pung.
Performed by Sondayam Ram De Borma and team members, Agartala. Tripura.
