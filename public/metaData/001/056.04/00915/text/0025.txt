Koli

Kaksar is performed by the Abhujmaria tribe of Bastar. It is a festival
dance performed to invoke Kaksar. the deity who presides over harvest
and rain. The most fascinating aspect of the dance is the use of tinkling
bells of different sizes tied to the waists of the dancers. Their sound
imparts a magical and soothing effect to the accompanying music. Both
boys and girls perform the dance and it provides the unmarried ones an
opportunity to choose their life partners.

The Koli dance carries with it the fragrance and aroma of sea life. The
Kolis inhabit the seashore and perform a variety of dances revolving
around the life of fishermen near the seashore.

Performed by Ramu Ram and team members, BasIar. Madhya Pradesh.

The dance is performed in distinct patterns and the dancers are
divided into two groups. The smaller of the two groups in male and

female pairs enacts the main theme of the dance in which the Kolin or
fisherwoman, makes overtures to the Koli or the fisherman. The larger
group also in male and female pairs forms two rows and continues to
perform a single sustained looped movement which imitates the tossing
and rowing of a boat on seawaves.
