Staging Macbeth very simply and in a small theatre does not
make the task any easier than it would have been in an elaborate
production. In many ways it is more difficult because an audience

sees everything and every actor can be closely scrutinized.
Instead of presenting a picture for an audience to view, I
wanted to create an event to be witnessed and, perhaps, shared.
Everyone, actors and audience, will be in the same light so that
what happens on stage, almost within touch, may be viewed as if
it were part of a waking dream. It will not be mistaken for life —
Shakespeare has seen to that - it is more sensational and l hope,

more revealing; its conflicts are more vivid and its feelings more
articulate, than those of our daily lives.
You will quickly see that we have not taken any great pains to
disguise the fact that these are young actors, many of whom are
playing persons of much greater age. We have tried to represent
difference in years but what we seek is not realism or an imitation
of actual behavior. In the
same spirit, we have not tried
to show that the actors and
the persons in the play are of
